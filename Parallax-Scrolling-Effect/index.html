About

Mount Everest is Earth's highest mountain, situated in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. Standing at 29,032 feet (8,848 meters), it was first conquered by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953. Climbing Everest is perilous due to thin air, extreme cold, and altitude sickness. The South Col route from Nepal is the most common ascent path. Everest Base Camp, located in Nepal, serves as the starting point for expeditions. Sherpas, indigenous to the Himalayas, play a vital role in supporting climbers. Environmental concerns and regulations aim to address the impact of tourism. Everest remains an iconic symbol in mountaineering and adventure.
